## The Company

**echo-Wisdom** — always written with lowercase *e* and a hyphen. Never "EchoWisdom", "Echo Wisdom", or "Echowisdom".

**One-liner:** The learning layer for higher education — helping educators turn their materials into complete, personalized learning experiences.

**What it does:** Educators create **Echos** — AI-powered learning companions grounded in their own content, voice, and pedagogy. Students get guided practice and reflection. Institutions get visibility and control.

## Limor's Outreach Style

Read the full templates and examples in `/workspace/extra/shaul-work/blurbs and reach outs.md`.

Key principles:
- **"Here to help" framing** — never "we're reimagining X". Echo-Wisdom is *here to help* educators do what they already want to do.
- **"Citizen" mindset** — she enters conversations wanting to learn and earn the seat at the table, not to demand it
- **Personal and warm** — references to specific things about the person, not generic pitches
- **Brief** — she doesn't write walls of text. Gets to the point fast.
- **Honest about traction** — mentions Fred Kofman's CBC, Brown, PennState naturally, not as name-drops
- **Asks for a short conversation (20 min)**, not a demo

**Three outreach scenarios:**
1. **Connector request** — asking someone to intro her to a target. Subject: "Intro to [Name]?"
2. **Direct reach-out** — to an expert, thought leader, or academic. Personal hook + value prop + short ask.
3. **Reconnection** — updating a past contact, often asking for an intro at the same time.
